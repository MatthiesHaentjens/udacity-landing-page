    Valuing your equipment. How does that work?

    Based on the brand, type, year of make and the meter read we estimate
    the current value of the equipment using a number of data sources, like

    • Our own database of equipment we have leased in the past
    • Valuation databases like Lectura, Mascus and EquipmentWatch who
      collect data on equipment that is being offererd online and that sold on auctions

    Historical data of comparable equipment only get you so far in the end
    each piece of equipment has its own unique characteristics. To perform
    a good assessment we need at least

    • The original invoice
    • List of repairs and maintenance
    • List of attachements
    • Photos showing the exterior, interior, meter reads and serial number
    • And everything else you think is relevant

    One of our equipment experts performs a detailed assessment based on the information provided.
    He will reach out with a short survey and if possible stops by to inspect the equipment in person

    Expect that the assessment will take-up 2 weeks. Once completed our equipment expert
    writes up a comprehensive valuation report, sends it to your email and suggests a
    date to schedule a follow-up call to discuss the valuation
